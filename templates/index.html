The Fate of Forests in the World's Largest Economies

This page decribes the evolution of forest cover in the world's largest economies. There are a mix of expected and surprising details.

As expected, Brazil's forest cover has decreased in the last few decades. This is concurrent with reports of widespread deforestation in the Amazon to make way for agriculture, particularly soy.

Surprisingly, forest cover in both India and China has increased in the last few decades, even though both these countries have been expanding their urban and agricultural reach. What is also surprising to me is that almost 70% of Japan is covered in forest.
